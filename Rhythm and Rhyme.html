CLASSICAL POETS
- William Shakespeare: Regarded as one of the greatest writers in the English language, known for his plays and poems that have been studied for centuries.
- William Wordsworth: A key figure in the English Romantic movement, celebrated for his nature-inspired poems like "Lines Composed a Few Miles above Tintern Abbey".
- John Keats: A prominent Romantic poet, famous for his odes like "Ode to a Nightingale" and "Ode on a Grecian Urn".

ROMANTIC POETS
- Emily Dickinson: A reclusive American poet known for her unique and enigmatic poems that explored themes of death, love, and nature.
- Robert Frost: A celebrated American poet, awarded four Pulitzer Prizes for his rural New England settings and exploration of human nature.
- Percy Bysshe Shelley: An English Romantic poet, famous for his poems like "Ozymandias" and "Prometheus Unbound".

MODERN POETS
- Langston Hughes: A prominent figure in the Harlem Renaissance, known for his powerful poems about the African American experience.
- Sylvia Plath: An American poet and novelist, famous for her intense and emotional poems that explored themes of mental illness and personal struggle.
- Pablo Neruda: A Chilean poet and Nobel laureate, celebrated for his passionate and evocative poems that explored love, politics, and the human condition.

CONTEMPORARY POETS
- Maya Angelou: An American poet, memoirist, and civil rights activist, known for her powerful poems about identity, racism, and personal empowerment.
- Tupac Shakur: An American rapper and poet, famous for his powerful and thought-provoking lyrics that explored themes of social justice and personal struggle.
- Shel Silverstein: An American poet, songwriter, and illustrator, celebrated for his whimsical and humorous poems that explored themes of childhood and imagination.
